Genetic Algorithms

Definition

Genetic Algorithm - A genetic algorithm is a metaheuristic inspired by the process of natural selection, commonly used to find solutions to optimization and search problems using mutation, crossover, and selection.

Stuff

Phenotype - "Collection of properties or attributes it exhibits during development." Made from genotype.

Formula:

\(\alpha = \beta\)

For a genetic algorithm to be "alive" -

  1. System must be able to directly replicate own instructions, and indirectly replicate machinery to execute them. (instructions for own replication)
  2. Replication must be fairly exact, but mutations should occur at a low rate.

Arguments

No mutation means no new genetic material, only recombinations of old. Too much mutation leads to chaos. "interesting systems balance on the edge of chaos."
